What is manga?
Manga is the Japanese word for comics published in Japan. In Japan, manga is published in manga magazines such as Shōnen Jump, multiple chapters at time, then republished into volumes. These tankōbon volumes are what you would see in your local library or book store. Manga is printed in black and white usually because of the expense.
Who makes manga?
Creators of manga are called mangaka. Mangaka are both author and illustrator of their works and each one has their own unique style of manga. Some examples of famous mangaka are Tezuka Osamu (“Astro Boy”), Akira Toriyama (“Dragon Ball Z”) and Naoko Takeuchi (“Sailor Moon”).
